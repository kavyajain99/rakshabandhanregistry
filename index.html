What is Rakhi & who celebrates?

Raksha bandhan, or Rakhi, is a popular Indian festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters. Sisters tie a decorative thread called a Rakhi around their brothers' wrists, symbolizing their love and wishes for their brothers' well-being. In return, brothers give gifts and promise to protect their sisters. The festival, usually held in August (re: the lunar calendar), highlights cousin/sibling ties and mutual respect.
